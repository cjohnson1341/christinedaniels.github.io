Wireframes

Onto the wireframes! During this stage, I really wanted to iterate as much as I could before investing in very time-heavy UI creation. The main point of the wireframing stage for me was to make sure the information flowed nicely from screen to screen for the user; I also wanted to make sure that I explored ways to let the user back out of options if they went somewhere they didn‚Äôt intend to go.

Before I even touched a computer, though, I wanted to get the rough ideas out of my head and onto paper. Even after doing this for a while, it‚Äôs amazing to me how a design can look so different in your head vs. when you put it down on paper; the human brain has an amazing way of hiding the flaws in your designs from you ü§£.

Originally, I was thinking this game would be more of a timed runner game, kind of like Temple Run, where you would have to dodge PMs and VPs to get your design delivered on time. But there were a few flaws with that idea, the major one being that I wanted the player to feel like they could collaborate with other designers and navigate the corporate world rather than feeling like a football player running a pass down the field. So that idea was scratched.

I then sketched out some ideas for a more open-world type game, where the world was an office building with a couple floors that could be navigated by the user. This felt more in line with the original game theme, so I settled on creating wireframes for that idea.

While wireframing, I ended up going back and doing more research on the open world games that I thought had minimal user interfaces. I also tried to keep my scope limited to just the initial user start up, since I was on a tight time schedule. I felt that focusing on this area would help me later down the road since many of the screens and UI components from an initial user onboarding can be reused in other screens.

3D Modeling

Because I had never tried my hand at 3D modeling before, I was really excited to give it a go. I decided that I would spend the bulk of my time learning how to do basic character modeling and lighting/rendering, since those would build a good knowledge base for me in the future if I went back to visit this game idea. For the office itself, I knew that it would take more time than I had to create all the assets I would need, so I found and downloaded a wonderful office model from lowpolyside on CGTrader.

UI High Fidelity

After settling on the final wireframes and picking two screens to do high fidelity renders on, I imported them into Photoshop and got to work. I started with the much more complicated play screen first, since it would help me work on a broader range of UI components that could be reused on the character creation screen.

Because my game was low-poly, I looked up examples of low-poly user interfaces and tried to mimic the style as best I could. I had a few moments where I had to start over because my UI was looking too flat, but in the end I‚Äôm happy with the designs. I also added a bit of ‚Äúweathering‚Äù to my UI components, and that seemed to make them stand out a bit more from the screen.
